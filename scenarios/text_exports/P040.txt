What if I’m not straight but I also don’t wanna label it, is that normal or am I just trying to be different?

That’s actually *really* normal. A lot of people your age (and older!) question their sexuality and don’t feel ready to put a label on it — or just don’t want one at all.

Not wanting a label doesn’t mean you’re “trying to be different.” It usually just means you’re still figuring things out, or you don’t feel like any one word fits you right now. And that’s completely okay.

Sexuality can be:
- Clear and certain for some people  
- Confusing and evolving for others  
- Something that changes over time  
- Something people choose not to define at all  

There’s no deadline to figure it out. You don’t owe anyone a label.

It might help to ask yourself:
- Am I feeling pressure to define this for other people?
- Do I actually want a label, or do I just feel like I *should* have one?
- What feels honest to me right now?

You’re not “trying to be different.” You’re trying to understand yourself. That’s a healthy and brave thing to do.
