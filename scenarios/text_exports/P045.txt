Hey. First — you are **not** being stupid.  
What you’re describing sounds a lot like anxiety or a panic surge, and those can feel *very* physical and very real. Tight chest, racing thoughts, feeling out of control — that’s your nervous system going into alarm mode. It doesn’t mean you’re weak or dramatic.

Right now, let’s focus on calming your body first. When your body settles, your thoughts usually follow.

Try this with me:

**1. Slow breathing (do it with me):**  
Inhale slowly through your nose for 4 seconds.  
Hold for 4 seconds.  
Exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds.  
Let your shoulders drop when you breathe out.  
Do that 5 times.

If your chest feels tight, longer exhales help signal your body that you’re safe.

**2. Grounding (to stop the spiral):**  
Name:
- 5 things you can see  
- 4 things you can feel  
- 3 things you can hear  
- 2 things you can smell  
- 1 thing you can taste  

This helps your brain shift out of panic mode.

Also — tight chest from anxiety is common, but if you have:
- Sharp or severe chest pain
- Trouble breathing that doesn’t ease
- Dizziness like you might pass out
- Or if something just feels seriously wrong

please tell a trusted adult or seek medical help right away. It’s always okay to get checked out.

Right now though, it sounds like your nervous system is overwhelmed, not that you’re “stupid.” Anxiety lies. It makes everything feel urgent and dangerous.
