How do I like actually budget my money if I get $20 sometimes and then I just spend it on snacks and skins and it’s gone?

That’s honestly super normal. When money comes in randomly, it’s way harder to manage. The fact you’re even thinking about this is actually really mature.

Here’s a simple way to handle it:

### 1. Split it immediately
When you get $20, don’t treat it like $20 to spend.

Try this:
- **$10 – Spend**
- **$5 – Save**
- **$5 – Future/Fun goal**

You can adjust the amounts, but the key is: **decide where it goes before you spend it.**

If you keep all $20 in one place, your brain goes “snacks + skins time.”

---

### 2. Use the “24-hour rule”
If you want something that isn’t small (like a skin or extra snacks), wait 24 hours.

Ask yourself:
- Do I still want this tomorrow?
- Is this better than saving for something bigger?

Half the time, the urge fades.

---

### 3. Make saving feel worth it
Saving is hard if there’s no goal.

Pick something:
- New game?
- Headphones?
- Clothes?
- Something big you’d actually be excited about?

Now every time you don’t buy snacks, you’re not “missing out.” You’re moving closer to that thing.

---

### 4. Make snacks a small budget
Instead of “I can’t buy snacks,” say:
“I have $5 for snacks this week.”

When it’s gone, it’s gone. That makes you more aware instead of mindless spending.

---

### 5. Real talk about skins
Skins feel awesome for like… 2 days.
Saving for something bigger usually feels good for way longer.

It’s not that skins are bad. It’s just about balance.
