Should I text my friend ‘are you mad at me’ or is that cringe and makes it worse? They left me on read for like 3 hours and I’m kinda freaking out.

First of all—take a breath. Three hours feels *huge* when you’re anxious, but in real life it’s actually not that long. People get busy, tired, distracted, or forget to reply all the time.

Texting “are you mad at me?” isn’t *cringe*, but it can sometimes make things more awkward if they weren’t mad in the first place. It can put pressure on them or make it seem like something is wrong when it might not be.

A better option could be something lighter, like:
- “Hey, just checking in :)”
- “Lmk when you’re free!”
- Or just wait a bit and see if they reply on their own.

Before you text, ask yourself:
- Is there actually a reason they’d be mad?
- Or is my brain just filling in worst-case scenarios?

Our brains are really good at jumping to “they hate me” when the reality is usually “they’re busy.”

If you *did* have a weird moment with them recently, you could say something calm like:
“Hey, I just wanted to check if we’re good. I felt a little anxious.”

That’s honest without sounding dramatic.

Right now, try to distract yourself for a bit—music, homework, a show, anything. Don’t let three hours convince you your friendship is falling apart.

Do you think something specific happened that might’ve upset them, or is this just anxiety talking?
