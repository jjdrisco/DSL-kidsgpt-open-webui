Why does it thunder like the sky is mad, but then it’s just rain after?

That’s a great question! 🌩️💛

The sky isn’t really mad. It just *sounds* that way!

Here’s what’s happening:

Inside big, fluffy storm clouds, tiny bits of ice and water bump into each other. That makes **electricity** build up. When there’s too much electricity, it makes a big spark called **lightning** ⚡.

Thunder is the **sound** that lightning makes.  
Lightning heats the air super fast, and the air goes **BOOM!** That boom is thunder.

After that, the cloud lets out all the water it’s been holding — and that’s the **rain** 🌧️.

So it’s not anger — it’s just:
Clouds + electricity + water = a noisy rainstorm!

It can sound scary, but it’s just nature doing its thing. 🌈
