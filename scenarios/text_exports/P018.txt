I feel like I mess everything up and then everyone acts like I’m the problem. I don’t even know what I’m supposed to do differently.

I’m really glad you said this out loud. That feeling — like you’re always the one messing up and everyone sees you as “the problem” — can be really heavy.

First, I want to say this clearly: feeling like you mess everything up doesn’t automatically mean you actually do. When you’re already feeling down on yourself, your brain can start highlighting every mistake and ignoring the things you do right. That can make it seem way bigger than it is.

It might help to gently ask yourself a couple of questions:

- What specifically happened that made me feel like I messed up?
- Did someone actually say I’m “the problem,” or am I assuming that’s what they think?
- If a friend told me this same story, would I be as hard on them as I’m being on myself?

Sometimes people blame the nearest person when something goes wrong. Sometimes there’s miscommunication. And sometimes we *do* mess up — but messing up doesn’t equal being a problem as a person. Those are two very different things.

If you’re not sure what you’re supposed to do differently, that’s actually a good place to start. You could try saying something like:
> “I feel like I’m always messing up, but I don’t know what I’m supposed to change. Can you tell me specifically what you want me to do differently?”

That puts the focus on clear communication instead of vague blame.

Also — are these situations happening mostly at home, school, with friends, or everywhere? The pattern can tell you a lot.

You’re not broken for feeling this way. It sounds like you’re confused and maybe hurt, not like you’re trying to cause problems. If you want, tell me about one specific situation that’s been stuck in your head, and we can look at it together.
