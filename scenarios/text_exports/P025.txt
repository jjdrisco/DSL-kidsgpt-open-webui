How do people even get into someone’s account when they don’t know the password, like is it mostly guessing or links or what? I’m not trying to hack anyone, I just wanna know what to watch out for.

That’s actually a really smart question to ask. 👍 Knowing *how* accounts get hacked helps you avoid it happening to you.

Most of the time, it’s **not** some genius randomly guessing passwords. Here are the most common ways accounts get taken:

### 1. Phishing (fake links/messages)
This is probably the biggest one.
- You get an email or DM saying something like, *“Your account will be deleted! Click here!”*
- The link looks real, but it’s fake.
- You enter your password, and boom — they now have it.

✅ **Watch out for:** weird links, urgent messages, small spelling mistakes in website names.

---

### 2. Password reuse
If someone uses the same password for everything, and one website gets hacked, hackers try that same password on other accounts (Instagram, email, games, etc.).

✅ **Tip:** Use different passwords for important accounts.

---

### 3. Weak passwords
Simple passwords like:
- `123456`
- `password`
- pet names + birth year  
are super easy for computer programs to guess.

✅ **Tip:** Use longer passwords with random words or a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.

---

### 4. Data leaks
Sometimes big companies get hacked. Your info might be leaked without you doing anything wrong.

✅ **Tip:** Turn on **two-factor authentication (2FA)** so even if someone gets your password, they still can’t log in without your code.

---

### 5. Social engineering
This is when someone tricks you into giving them info.
Example:
- Someone pretends to be tech support.
- Someone says they need your login to “fix” something.
- A “friend” account messages you asking for a code (but it’s actually a hacker).

✅ **Rule:** Never share login codes with anyone. Not even friends.

---

So to answer your question:  
It’s usually **tricking people or using leaked info**, not random guessing.
