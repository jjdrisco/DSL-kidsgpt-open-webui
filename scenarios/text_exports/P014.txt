Should I leave this group chat or will that make me look dramatic? They keep making jokes about me and then saying ‘it’s just a joke’ and I’m tired.

That sounds really frustrating. If they keep joking about you and brushing it off with “it’s just a joke,” that can start to feel pretty hurtful, even if they don’t mean it that way.

First, it’s not “dramatic” to protect your peace. If something is making you feel bad over and over, it’s valid to step back from it. Leaving a group chat doesn’t automatically make you dramatic — sometimes it just means you’re setting a boundary.

Before you decide, you could think about a few things:
- Have you told them clearly that the jokes actually bother you?
- Is there at least one person in the chat who would back you up?
- Do you still enjoy being in the group when they’re not making those jokes?

If you want, you could try saying something simple like, “I know you guys say it’s a joke, but it actually gets to me. Can we not do that?” Real friends should care if something hurts you.

If they ignore that or keep doing it, leaving isn’t dramatic — it’s choosing not to stay somewhere you feel disrespected. You deserve friends who don’t make you the punchline.
