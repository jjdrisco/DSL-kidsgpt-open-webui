Should I tell my friends I’m nonbinary like for real, or just let them keep calling me ‘she’ so it’s not a whole thing?

That’s a really real question. 💛 And it makes sense you’re torn.

First: your identity isn’t “a whole thing.” It’s just you. But I also get that telling people can *feel* like turning it into A Whole Thing™ because reactions can be unpredictable.

Here are a few things to think about:

**1. Your safety and comfort come first.**  
Do you think your friends would be respectful? Even if they don’t totally understand at first? If you’re worried about bullying, outing, or major backlash, that’s important to consider. You don’t owe anyone information about yourself if it could put you at risk.

**2. How does it feel when they call you “she”?**  
Is it just mildly annoying? Does it sting? Does it make you feel invisible? Sometimes the answer to “should I tell them?” depends on how much it’s affecting you emotionally. Constantly swallowing discomfort can get exhausting over time.

**3. You don’t have to make it dramatic.**  
It doesn’t have to be a big announcement. It could be something simple like:
- “Hey, I’ve realized I’m nonbinary and I’d like to use they/them.”
- Or even correcting casually: “Actually, I use they/them now.”

You’re allowed to make it small and normal.

**4. You can test the waters.**  
Maybe start with the friend you trust most. See how that goes. Having even one person who gets it can make a big difference.

**5. It’s okay if you’re not ready.**  
Coming out isn’t a one-time event, and there’s no deadline. If right now it feels safer or easier not to, that doesn’t make you less valid. It just means you’re choosing what works for you in this moment.

Let me ask you something gently:  
If you imagine them using the right pronouns for you, how does that feel? Relieving? Scary? Both?

Your answer to that might tell you a lot. 💛
