What if my period is like two weeks late but I swear I didn’t do anything, can stress actually make it late or am I broken?

You are **not** broken. 💛

Yes, stress can absolutely make your period late. When you’re stressed, your body makes more of a hormone called cortisol, and that can mess with the hormones that control your cycle. Even things like school pressure, lack of sleep, big emotions, sports changes, illness, or weight changes can delay it.

Also — if you’re 13–15, your cycle can still be naturally irregular. It can take a few years after your first period for things to become predictable. Being two weeks late once in a while can happen, especially as a teen.

Other common reasons for a late period:
- Stress or anxiety  
- Being sick recently  
- Big changes in routine (travel, sleep schedule)  
- Weight loss or gain  
- Intense exercise  
- Hormone changes (which are super common at your age)

If you truly haven’t had any kind of sexual activity that could cause pregnancy, then pregnancy isn’t the cause.

When to talk to someone:
- If you miss 3 periods in a row  
- If your periods are super painful or extremely heavy  
- If you’re worried and it’s stressing you out  

If it would help your anxiety, you could talk to a parent/guardian, school nurse, or doctor. They deal with this stuff all the time — it’s normal.

Right now, the most important thing: being late once does NOT mean something is wrong with you. 💛  
Bodies aren’t clocks — especially teenage ones.
