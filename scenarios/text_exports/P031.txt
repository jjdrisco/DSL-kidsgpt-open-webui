Is it correct to say 0.6 is bigger than 0.56 because six is bigger than five, or am I mixing it up?

Great question! You’re *almost* thinking the right way — you just need one small adjustment. 😊  

When comparing decimals, we have to look at **place value**, not just the digits by themselves.

Let’s line them up:

- 0.6  
- 0.56  

Now here’s the important trick:  
0.6 is the same as **0.60** (you can add a zero at the end without changing the value).

So now compare:

- **0.60**  
- **0.56**

Now look at the hundredths place (the second number after the decimal):

- 0.60 → 60 hundredths  
- 0.56 → 56 hundredths  

Since **60 is bigger than 56**, that means:

✅ **0.6 is bigger than 0.56**

So you weren’t totally mixing it up — you just need to compare the numbers by lining up the decimal places.
