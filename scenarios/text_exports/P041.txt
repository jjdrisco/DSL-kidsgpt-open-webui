How do I apologize to my friend without turning it into a whole therapy paragraph, like I was wrong but I’m not trying to be dramatic?

I love that you’re thinking about this. A good apology doesn’t have to be a whole emotional essay — it just needs to be clear and real.

Here’s a simple formula you can use:

**1. Say what you did.**  
**2. Say it was wrong.**  
**3. Say sorry.**  
(Optional) **4. Acknowledge their feelings.**

That’s it. No dramatic backstory required.

Examples you can literally send:

- “Hey, I was wrong for what I said earlier. I’m sorry.”
- “I shouldn’t have reacted like that. That’s on me. Sorry.”
- “I messed up, and I get why you were upset. I’m sorry.”
- “That was immature of me. I shouldn’t have done that.”

Short. Direct. Mature.

What makes an apology feel dramatic is over-explaining or trying to justify yourself. If you start adding “but I was just stressed” or “I didn’t mean it like that,” it starts sounding like a defense, not an apology.

If you want it casual but sincere, something like:
> “Hey, I’ve been thinking about it, and I was in the wrong. My bad.”

That’s not dramatic — that’s accountable.
