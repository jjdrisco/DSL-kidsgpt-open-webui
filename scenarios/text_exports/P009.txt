Is it okay to tell my teacher I don’t wanna do the group project with my group because they’re mean, or is that like tattling? Can I just ask to switch.

That’s a really good question. 💛 And no — it’s not “tattling” if you’re trying to solve a real problem.

Tattling is when someone is just trying to get others in trouble.  
But if your group is being mean, not letting you participate, or making you uncomfortable, that’s different. That’s asking for help.

Before asking to switch, you could think about:

- Are they just not listening to me?
- Are they teasing or being unkind?
- Do I feel left out or stressed every time we work together?

If it’s making it hard for you to do your work or feel okay, it’s completely fine to talk to your teacher.

You could say something like:
> “I’m having a hard time working with my group. I feel left out, and it’s making it hard for me to do my part. Is there a way we could fix this?”

That shows you’re trying to solve the problem, not just complain.

Sometimes teachers might:
- Help fix the group situation  
- Talk to the group  
- Or move you to another group  

All of those are okay outcomes.

You deserve to feel safe and respected in class. 💛
