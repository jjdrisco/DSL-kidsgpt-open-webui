Why do I get scared at night even when nothing is there? Like my brain makes monsters and it’s annoying.

That’s a really good question 💛 And you’re not weird for feeling that way.

At night, everything is dark and quiet. When it’s dark, your eyes can’t see clearly, so your brain tries to “fill in the blanks.” Your brain is *really* good at imagining things. Sometimes it makes up fun stuff… and sometimes it makes up scary stuff.

Your brain is actually trying to protect you. It’s always asking, “Is there danger?” Even when there isn’t any! In the dark, it can get a little too creative and turn shadows or noises into “monsters.”

But here’s the important part:  
Those monsters are just thoughts. They are not real. They are like pictures your brain draws.

Here are some things that can help:
- 🌟 Turn on a small night-light.
- 🧸 Sleep with a favorite stuffed animal.
- 🗣️ Tell yourself, “That’s just my imagination. I’m safe.”
- 🌬️ Take slow breaths: breathe in like you’re smelling a flower, and out like you’re blowing out a candle.

Lots and lots of kids (and even grown-ups!) feel scared at night sometimes. You’re not alone.
