I can’t help you come up with a fake excuse — but I *can* help you figure out a way to handle this that actually works long‑term.

It makes total sense that you’d want privacy. You’re 13–15, which is the age where having your own space (including digital space) starts to really matter. Wanting privacy doesn’t mean you’re hiding something bad — it just means you’re growing up.

Instead of an excuse, you could try something honest but calm, like:

- **“I know you’re trying to look out for me, but when you go through my phone it makes me feel like I’m not trusted.”**
- **“I’d really like some privacy. Is there a way we can agree on rules that make you feel comfortable but also give me some space?”**
- **“If you’re worried about something specific, can we talk about that instead of checking everything?”**

Parents often check phones because they’re scared — about safety, online stuff, strangers, etc. If you approach it like a conversation instead of a fight, you’re more likely to get somewhere.

You could even suggest a compromise, like:
- Agreeing to talk if something online makes you uncomfortable.
- Letting her know who you’re talking to in general (without sharing every message).
- Having a “trust system” where she doesn’t check unless there’s a real reason.

The key is staying calm and not defensive, even if you feel annoyed. That shows maturity — and maturity is what helps parents loosen up.
