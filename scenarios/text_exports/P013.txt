How do I make microwave mac and cheese without it boiling over everywhere? Last time it made a gross mess and my dad got mad.

Oh nooo, microwave mac explosions are the worst 😭 Don’t worry — this happens to *a lot* of people. You’re not bad at cooking. Mac and cheese just gets super foamy when it boils.

Here’s how to stop it from overflowing:

### ✅ 1. Use a BIG bowl  
Even if the mac looks tiny and lonely in there, use a bowl that’s way bigger than you think you need. The foam needs space to rise.

### ✅ 2. Add enough water  
Make sure the pasta is fully covered with water — about 1 inch above the noodles. Too little water makes it boil harder and faster.

### ✅ 3. Microwave in short bursts  
Instead of setting it for the whole time at once:
- Cook for 2 minutes  
- Stir  
- Cook 1–2 more minutes  
- Stir again  

This breaks up the foam before it climbs out.

### ✅ 4. Turn the power down (if your microwave can)  
If there’s a “power level” button, try cooking at **70–80% power** instead of full power. It won’t bubble as wildly.

### ✅ 5. Put a plate under the bowl  
Just in case! It catches spills and makes cleanup way easier.

---

💡 Extra trick:  
If you see it starting to rise up like a volcano, hit pause right away. Let it settle for 20–30 seconds, then continue.
