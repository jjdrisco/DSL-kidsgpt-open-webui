Why do parents fight and then act normal in the morning like it didn’t happen, are they gonna split up?

I’m really glad you asked this. It can feel confusing and even scary when parents argue and then act like everything is normal the next day.

First, it’s important to know: **parents arguing does not automatically mean they’re going to split up.** Lots of grown-ups disagree sometimes. Arguing is one way adults work through problems, even if it doesn’t look or sound nice.

Sometimes parents act normal the next morning because:
- They talked more after you went to bed and worked it out.
- They decided to handle it privately.
- They don’t want their kids to feel worried.
- They’re still figuring it out but don’t want the whole house to feel tense.

Adults don’t always explain what’s going on, and that can leave you feeling confused. But their disagreements are **not your fault**, and it’s not your job to fix them.

If you’re feeling worried about them splitting up, it’s okay to gently ask something like:
“Hey, I heard you arguing last night and it made me feel worried. Are things okay?”

You deserve to feel safe and to understand what’s happening around you.
